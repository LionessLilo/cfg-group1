Reshma Saujani:
A Trailblazer for Women in Tech

Reshma Saujani is not just a name in the world of tech, advocacy, and leadership—she's a force of nature. As the founder of Girls Who Code, an international nonprofit organization aimed at closing the gender gap in technology, Saujani has dedicated her life to empowering young women to excel in fields traditionally dominated by men. Her journey is a testament to the power of perseverance, passion, and purpose, serving as an inspiration for women around the globe.

Teach girls bravery, not perfection.

— Reshma Saujani
Founder of Girls Who Code

Reshma Saujani's story is one of resilience and redefinition. Born to Indian immigrants who fled Uganda's political turmoil in the 1970s, Saujani grew up in Chicago, deeply influenced by her parents' journey of struggle and survival. Their experiences instilled in her a sense of duty to fight for social justice and to ensure that marginalized voices were heard. This sense of purpose led her to Yale Law School and a career in public service.

However, Saujani's life took an unexpected turn when she decided to run for Congress in 2010. She knew the odds were against her, but she was driven by a desire to bring more diversity to the political arena. Although she lost that race, the experience was transformative. Saujani realized that her passion lay not just in politics but in addressing the systemic inequalities that kept women, particularly women of color, from achieving their full potential.

In 2012, Saujani founded Girls Who Code, an organization that would become the centerpiece of her legacy. The tech industry, known for its innovation, was ironically stuck in the past when it came to gender diversity. Women, particularly young girls, were often discouraged from pursuing careers in technology, and those who did often faced significant barriers. Saujani's vision was simple yet revolutionary: to inspire, educate, and equip girls with the computing skills necessary to pursue 21st-century opportunities. What began as a pilot program in New York City has now grown into a global movement, reaching hundreds of thousands of girls across the United States and around the world. Girls Who Code not only teaches coding but also fosters a sense of sisterhood and confidence among its participants. The program encourages girls to take risks, embrace failure, and support one another—values that Saujani believes are crucial to succeeding in any male-dominated field. Through her leadership, Saujani has not only helped to bridge the gender gap in technology but has also redefined what it means to be a leader in the 21st century.

One of Saujani's most powerful messages is the idea of "failing fast and failing forward." In her book, Brave, Not Perfect, she challenges the cultural conditioning that teaches women to strive for perfection, often at the expense of their happiness and success. Saujani argues that the fear of failure holds women back from taking risks, whether in their careers, relationships, or personal growth. Drawing from her own experiences, Saujani encourages women to embrace imperfection and to view failure as an essential part of the growth process. Her advocacy for fearlessness is not about recklessness; it's about understanding that failure is a stepping stone to success. By sharing her journey of setbacks and comebacks, Saujani has created a narrative that resonates with millions of women who struggle with self-doubt and the pressure to be perfect.

Reshma Saujani's influence extends far beyond Girls Who Code. She is a TED speaker, an author, and a role model who continues to challenge the status quo. Her work has earned her numerous accolades, including being listed on Fortune's World's Greatest Leaders and Forbes' Most Powerful Women Changing the World. But perhaps her most significant impact is in the way she has redefined success for women. Saujani's story teaches us that success is not a straight path; it's a journey filled with detours, challenges, and opportunities for growth. She advocates for a new kind of feminism—one that celebrates bravery over perfection, risk over safety, and sisterhood over competition.

Reshma Saujani's legacy is one of empowerment, resilience, and transformation. She has shown the world that one person's vision can spark a global movement, and that the power of purpose can overcome even the most daunting challenges. Through her work, Saujani has given countless girls the tools they need to succeed, not just in technology, but in life. As we look to the future, Saujani's message is clear: We must continue to challenge the barriers that hold women back, support one another in our journeys, and embrace the courage to be imperfect. In doing so, we can create a world where women are not just participants in the conversation, but leaders, innovators, and change-makers. Reshma Saujani's story is a beacon of hope and inspiration for women everywhere, reminding us that with passion, purpose, and perseverance, there is no limit to what we can achieve.
